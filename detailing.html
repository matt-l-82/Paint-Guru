Paint decontamination detail - prices from £80

This procedure removes organic and inorganic fallout from your vehicle’s surfaces. The way to tell if your paint work is contaminated is by running your hand over the surface, if it is not as smooth as glass and is rough then there are contaminants embedded on the surface. The vehicle will go through a thorough wash process to ensure that no further damage is inflicted onto the surfaces of the vehicle.


- Vehicle is rinsed in a comprehensive multi-stage process
- Vehicle is washed with a deep pile wash mitt and 2 bucket method
- Wheel arches cleaned
- Wheels are cleaned with non-acidic wheel cleaner
- Vehicle and wheels are treated to a iron fallout remover
- Vehicle and wheels are treated with a tar remover
- Vehicle paintwork is clayed to remove any further bonded contaminants
- Vehicle is wiped down with appropriate panel wipe and isopropal wipe
- Glass is cleaned inside and out
- Tyres are cleaned and dressed
- Final inspection

Stage One Detail – Prices from £200

A stage one detail is designed to increase the gloss of your paintwork that has been damaged by poor wash techniques and general wear and tear. The swirls you see in the paintwork are significantly reduced and deeper marks/scratches are reduced to the eye. This is the entry-level detail and will reduce the defects in the paint for older cars by 60-70% dependant upon condition, or even a top up of your vehicle has been detailed within the last 12-18 months.

Stage Two Detail – Prices from £350

When you have severe defects in your paint caused by very poor washing by the self-service automated car washes, water etching, birdlime etching, minor scuffs from car parks. These marks can reduce the depth of colour and rob the reflection and gloss in the paint. Darker paint will emphasis these defects more that lighter colour vehicles, even though the defects are there in the paint. Dependant upon the health of your paint your vehicle will go through a 2 stage paint correction that will start off with a cutting compound to remove the defects within the paint and followed by a lighter polishing stage to increase the gloss and clarity levels within the paint. These stages are carried out with care and precision to ensure that the minimum amount of clear coat is removed to improve the clarity. Machining takes an average of 25-35 hours alone to achieve a finish that leaves a 80% defect free finish.


- Vehicle is rinsed in a comprehensive multi-stage process
- Vehicle is washed with a deep pile wash mitt and 2 bucket method
- Wheel arches cleaned
- Wheels are cleaned with non-acidic wheel cleaner
- Vehicle and wheels are treated to a iron fallout remover
- Vehicle and wheels are treated with a tar remover
- Vehicle paintwork is clayed to remove any further bonded contaminants
- Paint is given a two stage paint correction
- Vehicle is wiped down with appropriate panel wipe and isopropal wipe
- Glass is cleaned inside and out
- Interior given a vacuum and wipe down
- Sealant applied to paintwork
- Dressing applied to exterior plastics
- Tyres are cleaned and dressed
- Final inspection

Stage Three Detail – Prices from £500

Following the Stage Two Detail this option benefits from added extras of engine bay detail and further comprehensive machine polishing. In order to achieve an 80-90% defect free finish the machining stage can take approximately 40-50 hours alone. To ensure that this finish is protected the paintwork is treated to multiple layers of high carnauba content wax or sealant.
